Wireless Technology Comparison

A professional overview of the four main wireless technologies used in modern computing environments.

Technology Usage Key Feature Advantages Limitations
Wi-Fi Connects computers, phones, and smart devices to the Internet in homes, offices, and public spaces. High speed, medium coverage.
  • Supports multiple devices at once.
  • Excellent for streaming, video calls, and online work.
  • Widely compatible with modern equipment.
  • Requires a router or access point.
  • Security depends on passwords and settings.
  • Signal can be weakened by walls or interference.
Bluetooth (BT) Connects nearby devices such as headphones, keyboards, mice, cars, and speakers. Low energy consumption, short range.
  • Easy to use and set up.
  • Saves battery on mobile devices.
  • Perfect for portable accessories.
  • Limited transfer speed.
  • Maximum range of about 10 meters.
  • Not suitable for Internet access.
NFC Contactless payments, electronic cards, and very short-distance data transfer. Extremely short range, fast authentication.
  • Very secure for financial transactions.
  • Quick and convenient for mobile payments.
  • No complex pairing required.
  • Works only within a few centimeters.
  • Cannot provide Internet access.
  • Limited to specific functions (payments, identification).
Cellular (4G/5G) Mobile connection for calls, messages, and Internet access on the go. Long distance, wide coverage.
  • Enables global communication without Wi-Fi.
  • Works while moving, with broad coverage.
  • High speed in 4G and especially 5G networks.
  • Depends on carrier coverage.
  • Can be expensive depending on data plans.
  • Consumes more battery compared to other technologies.
